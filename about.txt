        <h2>Overview</h2>
        <p>
            Navaratri is a Hindu festival that spans nine nights and is celebrated in the autumn every year.
            It is observed for different reasons and celebrated differently in various parts of the Indian
            subcontinent. The festival is associated with the prominent battle that took place between
            Goddess Durga and the demon Mahishasura.
        </p>
    </section>

    <section id="history">
        <h2>History</h2>
        <p>
            The history of Navaratri can be traced back to ancient mythology and legends. It symbolizes the
            triumph of good over evil, as the goddess Durga defeated the demon Mahishasura after a fierce
            battle that lasted for nine nights and ten days.
        </p>
    </section>

    <section id="celebrations">
        <h2>Celebrations</h2>
        <p>
            Navaratri is celebrated with great enthusiasm and devotion. People participate in traditional
            dance forms like Garba and Dandiya, wear colorful traditional attire, and create vibrant
            decorations. Temples are decorated, and religious ceremonies are performed during this auspicious
            time.
        </p>
    </section>

    <section id="rituals">
        <h2>Rituals</h2>
        <p>
            The festival involves various rituals and customs, including fasting, prayer, and the recitation
            of religious texts. Devotees visit temples dedicated to Goddess Durga and seek her blessings
            during this auspicious period.
        </p>
